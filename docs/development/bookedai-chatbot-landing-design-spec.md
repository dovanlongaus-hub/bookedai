# BookedAI.au Chatbot Landing Page Design Spec

## Purpose

Define the visual theme, messaging hierarchy, content structure, and interaction model for a refreshed `BookedAI.au` landing page focused on a chatbot platform experience.

This spec intentionally combines:

- the strongest structural ideas from the AI chatbot platform reference
- the existing `BookedAI.au` revenue-engine positioning
- a more trust-oriented, commercial UX than a generic developer AI product

## Core Message

### Primary headline direction

`The AI Revenue Engine that turns any kind of conversations into Your Revenue`

### Refined homepage headline recommendation

`The AI Revenue Engine That Turns Conversations Into Revenue`

### Supporting subheadline

`BookedAI.au captures enquiries across chat, calls, forms, email, and follow-up, then guides them toward qualified next steps, bookings, and measurable revenue.`

### Message rules

- Always connect AI conversation handling to a commercial outcome.
- Avoid broad claims like "AI for everything".
- Avoid over-emphasizing internal tooling, docs, or developer workflows on the main landing page.
- Prefer language like `booking`, `qualified lead`, `follow-up`, `conversion`, `revenue recovered`, and `booked outcome`.
- Speak to service businesses first, not to AI enthusiasts.

## Design Direction

### Chosen theme

`Conversational Enterprise Minimalism`

This theme should feel:

- intelligent
- calm
- premium
- credible for real businesses
- visibly AI-native without feeling neon or crypto-like

This theme should not feel:

- playful consumer chatbot
- devtool dashboard first
- futuristic sci-fi
- crowded SaaS template

## Visual System

### Color strategy

Use neutral tones as the base with a controlled AI-purple accent.

#### Base neutrals

- `--bg-main: #0B1020`
- `--bg-soft: #121826`
- `--surface: rgba(255,255,255,0.06)`
- `--surface-strong: rgba(255,255,255,0.10)`
- `--border: rgba(255,255,255,0.10)`
- `--text-primary: #F8FAFC`
- `--text-secondary: #CBD5E1`
- `--text-muted: #94A3B8`

#### Purple accent

- `--accent-purple-500: #8B5CF6`
- `--accent-purple-400: #A78BFA`
- `--accent-purple-300: #C4B5FD`
- `--accent-purple-glow: rgba(139,92,246,0.22)`

#### Supporting accents

- Trust green: `#22C55E`
- Warm signal amber: `#F59E0B`
- Optional support blue for data detail: `#4F8CFF`

### Background treatment

- Keep the background dark-neutral.
- Use one or two very soft radial glows.
- Purple glow should sit near hero and CTA areas only.
- Do not fill the whole page with colorful gradients.

### Surface treatment

- Cards should use soft glass or matte-dark surfaces.
- Surfaces need clear borders and separation.
- Blur should be subtle and never overpower readability.
- Hero preview card should feel like a product screenshot, not decorative glass art.

## Typography

### Recommended pairing

- Display: `Space Grotesk`
- Body: `DM Sans`

### Typographic behavior

- Headlines should be bold, compact, and high contrast.
- Paragraphs should remain calm and readable.
- Eyebrows and metadata can use tracked uppercase styling sparingly.
- Avoid giant marketing copy blocks wider than comfortable reading width.

## Page Objectives

The landing page must accomplish these goals quickly:

1. Explain what BookedAI.au is in under one screen.
2. Show a conversational product preview immediately.
3. Make the revenue angle feel concrete, not abstract.
4. Build trust through integrations, capabilities, and outcome framing.
5. Push users toward a clear primary CTA: `Try Now`.

## Information Hierarchy

### Above the fold priorities

1. Headline
2. Revenue outcome subheadline
3. Primary CTA
4. Conversational UI preview with streaming text animation
5. A short trust strip showing channels or integrations

### Mid-page priorities

1. AI capability cards
2. Conversation-to-revenue explanation
3. Integration logos
4. Example workflow or conversation demo

### Lower-page priorities

1. Objection handling
2. Commercial trust
3. Final CTA

## Section Architecture

## 1. Hero Section

### Goal

Introduce BookedAI.au as a revenue-generating conversational platform, not just a chatbot.

### Layout

- Two-column layout on desktop
- Single-column stack on mobile
- Left column: message and CTA
- Right column: conversational UI preview

### Hero content

- Eyebrow: `BookedAI.au Chat Revenue Platform`
- Headline: `The AI Revenue Engine That Turns Conversations Into Revenue`
- Supporting text focused on enquiries, booking paths, and revenue visibility
- Primary CTA: `Try Now`
- Secondary CTA: `See Live Demo` or `How It Works`

### Hero preview

The preview should resemble a live booking assistant conversation.

Recommended flow:

- User asks a realistic service-business question
- Assistant responds with streamed text
- Assistant clarifies the request
- Assistant suggests next step such as booking, callback, quote, or follow-up
- A small outcome rail shows `Qualified`, `Booked`, or `Revenue opportunity detected`

### Hero animation

- Primary motion is streaming text reveal
- Include a cursor blink during stream
- Optional typing dots appear before streamed reply
- Animation must loop gently or stop in a polished resting state

## 2. Conversational Value Strip

### Goal

Show that BookedAI.au handles more than website chat.

### Content direction

Use pill items such as:

- Website chat
- Missed calls
- SMS follow-up
- Email enquiries
- Booking requests
- Lead recovery

This section should feel like capability proof, not a dense feature list.

## 3. Capability Cards

### Goal

Explain what the AI does in business terms.

### Card themes

- Instant AI Replies
- Lead Qualification
- Booking Guidance
- Follow-Up Recovery
- Multi-Channel Capture
- Revenue Visibility

### Card behavior

- Use icon + title + one-sentence benefit
- Keep copy short and commercial
- Hover state can slightly brighten border and raise card

## 4. Conversation Demo Section

### Goal

Show a more detailed example of how a real conversation becomes a revenue event.

### Recommended format

- Tabbed use cases or segmented scenarios
- Example scenarios: salon, clinic, tutor, trades
- Left side: user enquiry
- Right side: AI response and next-step logic

### Output framing

End each scenario with a visible outcome such as:

- Booking confirmed
- Callback requested
- Quote path opened
- Payment step triggered

## 5. Integration Logos Section

### Goal

Increase trust and reduce perceived implementation risk.

### Style

- Neutral grayscale logos by default
- Slight purple tint or full color on hover
- Clean grid with generous spacing

### Logo candidates

- Google Calendar
- Stripe
- Twilio
- HubSpot
- Zapier
- Meta
- WhatsApp
- Gmail or Outlook

Only show logos relevant to the actual product direction.

## 6. Outcome Framing Section

### Goal

Translate conversations into commercial outcomes.

### Recommended presentation

Three or four panels:

- More qualified conversations
- Faster booking conversion
- Fewer missed opportunities
- Better revenue visibility

This section should connect product mechanics to business value without becoming a pricing table.

## 7. Final CTA Section

### Goal

Close with a simple, high-confidence action.

### Content

- Headline focused on starting conversations that convert
- Primary CTA: `Try Now`
- Secondary CTA optional: `Book a Demo`
- Short reassurance line such as `Built for service businesses. Designed for measurable outcomes.`

## UX Principles

### Clarity over cleverness

- Users should understand the business outcome in seconds.
- Avoid poetic AI copy that hides what the product does.

### Product-led trust

- Show the product in action early.
- Prefer realistic conversation examples over abstract shapes.

### Commercial specificity

- Tie the interface to booking and revenue states.
- Use labels like `Qualified`, `Follow-up sent`, and `Booked`.

### Calm sophistication

- Motion should feel deliberate.
- Accent purple should guide attention, not dominate the page.

## Interaction Rules

### Buttons

- Primary button uses purple accent
- Primary CTA text should be `Try Now`
- Secondary buttons should be neutral, outlined, or soft-glass

### Hover states

- Lift cards by a few pixels at most
- Increase border contrast slightly
- Avoid heavy spring effects

### Scroll behavior

- Staggered reveal animation is acceptable
- Keep animation duration short and clean
- Never delay core content visibility too aggressively

## Mobile Behavior

- Hero stacks with copy first, preview second
- CTA buttons become full-width or near full-width
- Chat preview remains readable without horizontal scrolling
- Integration grid becomes 2-column or horizontally scrollable
- Feature cards stack cleanly with generous spacing

## Content Examples

### Recommended hero preview example

User:
`I missed your call earlier. Can I still book a consultation this week?`

Assistant:
`Yes. I can help with that. Are you looking for the earliest available consultation, or a specific day this week?`

Outcome tags:

- `Recovered lead`
- `Booking intent detected`
- `Next step ready`

### Recommended capability language style

Good:

- `Qualify leads before your team steps in`
- `Turn after-hours enquiries into next-day revenue`
- `Guide customers to the right booking path`

Avoid:

- `Unlock next-gen conversational intelligence`
- `Agentic orchestration for omnichannel synergy`

## Success Criteria

The page is successful when a first-time visitor can immediately answer:

1. What is BookedAI.au?
2. What kind of conversations does it handle?
3. How does it help generate revenue?
4. What should I do next?

## Implementation Notes

- The current site already has a dark premium foundation and suitable fonts.
- The main redesign shift is structural:
  move hero emphasis from revenue dashboard cards to conversational UI preview.
- Purple should become the primary marketing accent, while blue and green become supporting tones.
