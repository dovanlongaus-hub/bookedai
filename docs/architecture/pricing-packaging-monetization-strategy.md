# Pricing, Packaging, and Monetization Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the official pricing and monetization strategy for BookedAI after the current positioning upgrade.

BookedAI is no longer priced as a generic software plan ladder.

BookedAI is now positioned as:

- the AI revenue engine for service businesses
- a system that should be paid for in a way that aligns with commercial outcomes

## Executive summary

### Official pricing model

- one-time setup fee
- ongoing commission based on successful bookings or revenue generated

### Official pricing positioning

We win when you win.

Setup once, then pay based on performance.

### Why this model fits BookedAI

- BookedAI is being sold on revenue and booking outcomes
- service businesses are more likely to adopt when risk feels lower
- commission aligns incentives more clearly than a flat software fee
- the model distinguishes BookedAI from cheap chatbot software

## Pricing principles

Pricing should be anchored to:

- bookings generated
- revenue generated
- missed revenue recovered
- commercial outcomes created

Pricing should not be anchored primarily to:

- token counts
- raw message count
- feature count alone
- generic seat count

## Commercial framing

BookedAI should be sold as:

- a revenue engine, not a chat tool
- a commercial system that improves conversion and follow-up
- a lower-risk investment because pricing is aligned to outcomes

## Packaging structure

### 1. Setup fee

#### Positioning

A one-time onboarding and implementation fee to build and launch the BookedAI revenue engine around the customer's workflow.

#### Includes

- AI voice and chat setup
- channel workflow design
- search-to-enquiry conversion flow setup
- email and follow-up workflow setup
- booking automation configuration
- calendar, CRM, and payment integrations
- revenue dashboard configuration
- testing and launch support

#### Packaging notes

- setup is scoped to the business workflow, not a generic template only
- higher-complexity businesses may require higher setup scope

### 2. Performance-based commission

#### Positioning

After launch, BookedAI earns based on the value it helps create through successful bookings or attributable revenue.

#### Allowed commission bases

- per successful booking
- percentage of attributable revenue
- hybrid variant where vertical needs require it

#### Commission customization factors

- business type
- average booking value
- sales or booking cycle
- channel mix
- implementation scope
- payment flow structure

## Why the model works

- lowers adoption risk
- keeps incentives aligned
- helps sales conversations stay outcome-led
- creates stronger ROI clarity

## Product requirements created by this pricing model

The system must support:

- revenue attribution visibility
- booking-linked reporting
- commission summary reporting
- auditable commercial drill-downs
- payment-state visibility

These are not optional extras.

They are required to support the pricing model credibly.

## Public pricing-page requirements

The public pricing section must explain:

- one-time setup fee
- success-based commission
- why the model is aligned
- that commission structure depends on the business model
- CTA to book a strategy demo

The public page must avoid:

- fake ROI claims
- generic three-tier SaaS pricing tables if they conflict with the commission model
- wording that makes the product feel cheap or commodity-like

## Tenant and admin requirements

The pricing strategy requires product support for:

- commission summary widget
- revenue generated reporting
- payment completion status
- attribution visibility
- admin reconciliation views for commission support

## Operational constraints

- commission logic must be explicit and reviewable
- attribution logic must be explainable
- finance-facing summaries must be exportable or inspectable
- special-case pricing should remain controlled, not ad hoc chaos

### Headline

### Supporting line

### Trust line
