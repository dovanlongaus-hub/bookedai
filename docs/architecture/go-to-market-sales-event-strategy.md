# Go-To-Market, Sales, and Event Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the official Prompt 17-level strategy for go-to-market execution, direct sales, demo conversion, and event-driven customer acquisition in `BookedAI.au`.

It is written for an early-to-growth stage startup selling to real Australian SMEs.

The goal is not to produce generic startup marketing theory. The goal is to give the founder and early commercial team a field-usable operating playbook that helps BookedAI generate qualified pipeline, run strong demos, convert faster, and close real paying customers.

This strategy inherits and aligns with:

- Prompt 1 product and revenue-engine direction
- Prompt 6 public growth surface direction
- Prompt 10 CRM, email, and revenue lifecycle direction
- Prompt 15 analytics and revenue intelligence direction
- Prompt 16 pricing, packaging, and monetization direction

BookedAI should not be sold as a generic AI tool.

BookedAI should be sold as:

- a revenue engine
- a lead-conversion engine
- a booking-reliability engine
- a lifecycle follow-up engine

## Section 1 — Executive summary

- Primary commercial goal:
  - win real SME customers in Australia and close real paid deals quickly
- Core GTM motion:
  - combine SEO, direct outreach, event-led selling, founder-led demos, referral loops, and partner channels
- Best near-term acquisition priority:
  - focus on high-pain operators already losing leads, missing bookings, or handling enquiries manually through website chat, Facebook, phone, WhatsApp, or inbox
- Best commercial framing:
  - “BookedAI helps you stop missing customers and convert more enquiries into bookings.”
- Best channel mix for the current stage:
  - direct outreach and events for immediate pipeline
  - SEO for compounding inbound demand
  - referral and partner channels for lower-CAC expansion
- Biggest GTM mistake to avoid:
  - selling BookedAI as vague “AI for business” instead of a system that brings more bookings and revenue
- Event strategy principle:
  - do not attend events for general networking
  - attend events to identify specific prospects, run a live product demo, collect next-step commitment, and follow up within 24 hours

## Section 2 — ICP

### Core ideal customer profile

BookedAI should target Australian service SMEs that:

- rely on inbound enquiries and bookings
- lose leads because response is slow, inconsistent, or manual
- already use a website, Facebook page, WhatsApp, phone, or inbox as enquiry channels
- do not yet have strong automation across lead capture, qualification, booking, follow-up, and reminders
- can feel the revenue impact of missed demand without needing a long education cycle

### Strong-fit verticals

- clinics
- salons and beauty businesses
- swim schools
- tutors and education providers
- trades and home services
- child activity centres
- allied health and appointment-led local operators

### Buying signals

- owner or manager complains about missed calls or missed messages
- website gets traffic but low booking conversion
- staff spend too much time answering repetitive enquiries
- current booking flow is fragmented across multiple channels
- business depends on fast response and trust before payment
- enquiries arrive after hours and are not handled well

### Tiering model

#### Tier 1 — High intent

Operators with active lead leakage and visible urgency:

- missing calls, chats, or DMs
- delayed replies
- poor lead follow-up
- no clear booking automation
- obvious frustration with front-desk load

Recommended motion:

- founder-led outreach
- fast diagnostic
- immediate live demo
- trial or paid pilot close

#### Tier 2 — Medium intent

Operators who already know they need better systems but are not yet in active pain escalation:

- using a scheduler but still leaking leads
- have partial CRM or inbox process
- curious about AI but need proof tied to revenue

Recommended motion:

- education plus ROI framing
- demo booked within a few days
- follow-up with use-case-specific example

#### Tier 3 — Exploratory

Operators interested in AI, growth, or automation but not yet convinced they have an urgent problem:

- attend innovation events
- click informational content
- ask broad questions about automation

Recommended motion:

- nurture via content, remarketing, and periodic check-ins
- do not overinvest founder selling time too early

### Buyer personas

- owner-operator:
  - cares about revenue, time, and simplicity
- operations manager:
  - cares about process reliability and staff workload
- front-desk or admin lead:
  - cares about reducing repetitive manual work
- growth-minded founder:
  - cares about conversion rate, lead response, and ROI

## Section 3 — Value proposition

### Core messaging principles

- lead with business pain
- quantify lost revenue where possible
- use simple language
- avoid technical AI jargon
- make the next step obvious

### Primary message options

- `Don't miss customers anymore.`
- `Convert visitors into bookings.`
- `AI that actually brings revenue.`
- `Turn missed enquiries into booked customers.`
- `BookedAI helps service businesses reply faster, qualify better, and book more customers.`

### Positioning statement

BookedAI is a revenue engine for service SMEs that captures inbound demand, matches people to the right service, guides them toward a safe booking path, and follows up so fewer customers drop off.

### Proof themes to repeat

- more bookings
- fewer missed leads
- faster response
- lower admin load
- better follow-up consistency
- easier buyer trust in the booking path

### Messaging to avoid

- generic “AI transformation” language
- technical model talk
- feature dumping without outcome
- “chatbot” as the primary frame

## Section 4 — Acquisition channels

### Channel priority for current stage

1. direct outreach
2. events
3. SEO
4. referrals
5. partnerships

### A. SEO

Role:

- build long-term inbound demand
- support outbound credibility
- capture problem-aware and local-intent buyers

Priority page families:

- industry plus location pages
- problem-solution pages
- conversion-focused use-case pages
- trust and booking-reliability pages
- comparison pages against generic chatbots or schedulers

Best content angles:

- reduce missed bookings
- convert website visitors into bookings
- after-hours lead capture
- how service SMEs can stop losing leads
- AI booking assistant for salons in Sydney
- AI enquiry handling for clinics in Melbourne

SEO rule:

- every page should support either:
  - direct commercial intent
  - strong objection handling
  - local search demand

### B. Direct outreach

Role:

- fastest path to pipeline while product is still early

Best prospect sources:

- Google Maps
- local business directories
- industry association member lists
- LinkedIn
- Facebook business pages
- businesses with outdated or weak enquiry flows

Outreach triggers:

- slow or no response from web leads
- weak website CTA
- no clear booking path
- many reviews mentioning response issues
- visible multi-channel fragmentation

Recommended direct-sales flow:

1. identify target business and pain clues
2. send short revenue-focused message
3. offer 10-minute live demo
4. show BookedAI on their exact use case
5. close to trial or next-step implementation call

### C. Events

Role:

- create high-trust conversations fast
- shorten time from first contact to demo
- get warm introductions and live reactions

Best event types:

- AI meetups with SME operators
- startup and founder events
- local chamber of commerce events
- small-business expos
- vertical industry events for clinics, beauty, education, trades, and local services

Event selection rule:

- choose events where real operators, owners, or decision-makers are present
- deprioritize events full of vendors talking to other vendors

### D. Referrals

Role:

- lower CAC
- create trust transfer
- improve close rate

Best referral sources:

- existing customers
- friendly founders
- consultants
- accountants
- local marketers
- web agencies

Referral offer direction:

- one month free
- setup discount
- small cash referral fee for qualified paying customers

### E. Partnerships

Role:

- scale distribution without building a large sales team too early

Best partner categories:

- web developers building sites for SMEs
- local marketing agencies
- CRM implementers
- booking or operations consultants
- adjacent SaaS providers without strong AI conversion flows

Partner pitch:

- BookedAI helps your clients convert more inbound demand
- you keep your core service relationship
- we plug into the revenue leak without replacing your work

## Section 5 — Sales funnel

### Standard funnel

Traffic -> Lead -> Demo -> Trial -> Paid -> Retention -> Referral

### Funnel definitions

- Traffic:
  - SEO visit, ad click, referral click, direct outreach response, or event conversation
- Lead:
  - contact captured through form, WhatsApp, email, event scan, or booked follow-up
- Demo:
  - live product walk-through tailored to the prospect's use case
- Trial:
  - time-bound hands-on period or lightweight pilot
- Paid:
  - active paying customer
- Retention:
  - customer remains active and sees value

### Conversion points

#### Landing page -> Lead

Need:

- clear pain-first headline
- single strong CTA per context
- low-friction form
- optional WhatsApp contact path
- proof that BookedAI drives revenue

#### Lead -> Demo

Need:

- fast reply
- use-case-specific outreach
- short scheduling step
- clear promise:
  - “I'll show how this could convert more of your enquiries.”

#### Demo -> Trial

Need:

- show exact workflow fit
- keep demo under 10 minutes, with a 2 to 3 minute wow moment early
- propose small next step
- avoid broad procurement language too soon

#### Trial -> Paid

Need:

- defined setup scope
- simple onboarding
- proof of time saved or leads captured
- light urgency
- clear commercial ask

### Funnel operating targets for early stage

- prioritize speed over volume vanity
- keep founder response time under 24 hours
- aim to move warm event or outreach leads to demo within 3 days
- avoid long “thinking about it” stages without a scheduled next step

## Section 6 — Event strategy

### Event objective

Go to events to leave with:

- qualified prospects
- booked demos
- immediate product walk-throughs
- follow-up commitments

Do not go mainly to “raise awareness.”

### Target personas at events

- business owners
- operations managers
- growth-minded local service founders
- agency partners serving SMEs
- vertical operators with obvious booking demand

### Event selection checklist

- are real SME decision-makers attending
- is there enough local-service density
- can live demo conversations happen on-site
- is the event small enough for real interaction
- will the audience understand lost-booking pain quickly
- can follow-up happen within 24 hours with context still warm

### Pre-event checklist

- phone and laptop fully charged
- stable demo URL ready
- short vertical-specific demo scenarios prepared
- QR code to demo booking form
- concise one-line explanation memorized
- contact capture sheet or CRM-ready form
- follow-up email and WhatsApp templates ready
- list of target attendees or target company types prepared

### Event flow

1. select the right event
2. identify likely prospects before or during the event
3. open with a pain-led question
4. deliver a 30-second pitch
5. offer a live 2 to 3 minute demo immediately
6. capture contact and next step
7. send follow-up within 24 hours

### Approach script

Opening questions:

- `How are you handling enquiries and bookings today?`
- `Do you ever miss leads after hours or when staff are busy?`
- `What usually happens when someone lands on your site but is not ready to call?`

Transition line:

- `We built BookedAI to help service businesses stop losing those customers and turn more enquiries into bookings.`

### Event anti-patterns

- talking to everyone with no qualification
- leading with AI buzzwords
- using slide decks instead of product
- failing to ask for the next step
- waiting too long to follow up

## Section 7 — Sales pitch

### 30-second pitch structure

- problem
- impact
- solution
- outcome
- CTA

### Base pitch

`A lot of service businesses lose customers because enquiries come in across the website, Facebook, phone, and WhatsApp, but no one replies fast or consistently enough to convert them. BookedAI helps capture those enquiries, guide people to the right service, and move them into a real booking path, so you miss fewer customers and generate more revenue. If you want, I can show you a live 2-minute demo right now.`

### Vertical-tailored pitch examples

#### Clinic

`Clinics often lose bookings when patients ask questions after hours or are unsure which service to choose. BookedAI helps answer those enquiries, guide them to the right option, and turn more website visitors into booked patients. I can show you how it looks in under 2 minutes.`

#### Salon

`Salons lose revenue when DMs, calls, and web enquiries sit too long or clients do not know which booking path to take. BookedAI helps capture those leads and convert them into real appointments faster. Want to see a quick demo?`

#### Swim school or tutor

`Parents often enquire outside business hours and drop off if the reply takes too long. BookedAI helps qualify the need, guide them to the right class or service, and push them toward booking while interest is still high. I can show it live if helpful.`

## Section 8 — Demo strategy

### Demo principles

- use product, not slides
- keep it fast
- tailor to the prospect's vertical
- show a visible before-and-after outcome
- end on a clear commercial next step

### Demo goal

The demo should make the prospect feel:

- this fits my business
- this is simple enough to adopt
- this can help me get more bookings

### 2 to 3 minute demo flow

1. state the use case:
   - “Let's say a customer lands on your site after hours and wants to book.”
2. input the customer need
3. show AI match or qualification flow
4. show booking options or routing path
5. show trust and clarity in the next step
6. show payment or follow-up path where relevant
7. close:
   - “This is the kind of flow that helps you convert more inbound demand.”

### Demo structure for longer 10-minute calls

1. confirm current enquiry flow
2. identify where leads are leaking
3. show the live product on their use case
4. explain how BookedAI fits into existing workflow
5. show first implementation scope
6. ask for trial or onboarding step

### Demo preparation library

Prepare ready-made demo scenarios for:

- clinic booking question
- salon appointment request
- swim-school class enquiry
- tutoring match request
- trades quote or job request

### Demo CTA options

- `Start a free trial`
- `Let's set this up for your business`
- `Book a deeper setup session`
- `Want me to map this to your current enquiry flow this week?`

## Section 9 — Conversion strategy

### Conversion principles

- remove friction
- ask for one next step
- frame around ROI
- keep urgency light but real
- make value visible early

### Landing-page conversion guidance

- one primary CTA above the fold
- simple supporting CTA for demo
- pain-first copy
- proof of commercial outcome
- short form with minimum required fields
- clear statement of who BookedAI is for

### Demo conversion guidance

- show their use case quickly
- ask what happens today when leads come in
- quantify missed opportunity in simple terms
- propose a small trial, not a giant transformation

### Trial conversion guidance

- keep onboarding narrow
- define what success looks like in week 1
- review results quickly
- ask for paid continuation before momentum cools

### Offer design for speed

- free trial
- implementation-light pilot
- clear first-month value framing
- selective referral or startup offer when it helps reduce hesitation

## Section 10 — Sales playbook

### Cold outreach script

Subject:

- `Are you missing enquiries after hours?`
- `Quick idea to convert more bookings`
- `BookedAI for [Business Name]`

Email body:

`Hi [Name],`

`I noticed that businesses like [Business Name] often receive enquiries across website, phone, or social channels, but not every lead converts when response is delayed or manual.`

`BookedAI helps service businesses capture those enquiries, guide customers to the right service, and turn more visitors into bookings.`

`If helpful, I can show you a 10-minute live demo based on your exact booking flow.`

`Best,`

`[Sender]`

### LinkedIn or DM script

`Hi [Name], we help service businesses stop missing inbound enquiries and convert more visitors into bookings. If you're open to it, I can send a quick 2-minute example for your use case.`

### Event script

- ask one pain question
- qualify whether they own bookings or operations
- deliver 30-second pitch
- offer immediate live demo
- capture contact plus next step

### Demo script

- `How are enquiries and bookings handled today?`
- `Where do customers drop off most often?`
- `Let me show how BookedAI would handle that flow.`
- `Would a setup like this be valuable if it helped you convert more enquiries?`
- `The easiest next step is a trial or a short implementation session.`

### Follow-up script

`Thanks for the chat today. Based on what you shared, the main opportunity seems to be converting more of your incoming enquiries into bookings, especially [pain point].`

`Here is the next step we discussed: [demo / trial / setup call].`

`If it helps, I can also tailor the flow specifically for [their business type].`

### Closing rules

- always ask for a next step
- never end with “let me know”
- propose a concrete time or action
- keep the ask small and specific

## Section 11 — Objection handling

### “We already have a system”

Response direction:

- do not attack the existing system
- focus on what still leaks revenue

Suggested response:

`That makes sense. In many cases the issue is not whether a business has a system, but whether the current setup is actually converting every enquiry into a booking. BookedAI usually sits on the revenue-leak side of the problem: faster response, better qualification, and better follow-up.`

### “We don't need AI”

Response direction:

- avoid debating AI
- return to commercial outcome

Suggested response:

`That's fine. The real question is not whether you want AI as a label, but whether you want fewer missed customers and a smoother booking flow. If the current process already converts well, great. If not, BookedAI is a practical way to fix that.`

### “It's too expensive”

Response direction:

- compare cost against missed revenue
- narrow the initial scope

Suggested response:

`Totally fair. Usually the comparison is not against software in general, but against the value of the bookings currently being missed. If one or two extra customers a month cover the cost, then it becomes much easier to justify. We can also start with a lighter rollout.`

### “It sounds hard to use”

Response direction:

- reduce perceived complexity
- show, do not explain

Suggested response:

`The easiest way to answer that is to show the live flow. The goal is not to give your team another complicated dashboard. The goal is to make it easier for customers to reach the right booking path and reduce manual follow-up work.`

## Section 12 — Follow-up

### Timing rules

- first follow-up within 24 hours
- second follow-up within 2 to 3 business days
- third follow-up within 7 days

### Recommended channels

- email
- WhatsApp where appropriate
- LinkedIn for lighter-touch reminders

### Follow-up structure

1. reference the exact conversation
2. restate the pain point
3. restate the value
4. propose one next step

### Follow-up examples

#### After event

`Great meeting you at [event name]. You mentioned that [pain point]. That's exactly where BookedAI can help by converting more enquiries into bookings. Are you free this week for a short tailored demo?`

#### After demo

`Thanks again for the demo today. Based on your current flow, the biggest opportunity looks like [pain point]. If you want, we can move straight into a simple trial so you can test this in a real booking scenario.`

### Follow-up rule

- every follow-up should reintroduce value
- do not send empty reminder messages

## Section 13 — Metrics

### Core weekly GTM metrics

- leads generated per week
- demos booked per week
- demos completed per week
- trials started per week
- paid conversions per week

### Core conversion metrics

- landing page to lead rate
- lead to demo rate
- demo to trial rate
- trial to paid rate
- event conversation to qualified lead rate
- outreach reply rate

### Financial metrics

- CAC
- payback period
- MRR added
- LTV
- expansion revenue

### Channel metrics

- SEO leads by page family
- event leads by event type
- direct outreach conversion by segment
- partner-sourced revenue
- referral conversion rate

### Operating rule

- if a channel creates meetings but not paid customers, fix the messaging or qualification
- if a channel creates qualified interest with low volume, scale it

## Section 14 — Final recommendations

- Sell BookedAI as a revenue engine, not as generic AI software.
- Put founder-led demos and direct sales at the center of the near-term motion.
- Use events as live-conversion environments, not networking exercises.
- Keep demos short, product-led, and specific to the prospect's use case.
- Use SEO to compound demand around industry, location, and pain-driven intent.
- Build lightweight referral and partner loops as soon as the first customers are happy.
- Track every conversion point from first contact to paid customer.
- Keep the commercial ask simple:
  - more bookings
  - fewer missed leads
  - faster response
  - easier follow-up

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- attending events with no prospect target
- long pitches
- slide-heavy demos
- vague AI messaging
- weak follow-up
- no close ask
- no metrics discipline

## Assumptions

- BookedAI is operating in an early-to-growth stage where founder-led sales is still an advantage.
- The current repo and documentation already support a revenue-engine positioning, public growth surface, consultation flow, and pricing strategy that can back this GTM motion.
- This document defines the official GTM execution baseline, even if not every CRM, attribution, or sales-ops workflow is fully automated yet.
